F1 Laptimes: Beeswarm Plot

My first time exploring D3's force layouts. d3-force's collision constraint helps to create closely-packed, non-overlapping points. The plot strips make it useful to compare laptimes between teams. I also created dropdown menus to allow viewers to choose a specific race to visualize.

F1 Laptimes: Scatterplot

A standard time vs lap number plot. Time = time taken to complete a lap. As time does not vary much between each lap and driver, this results in extremely closely spaced or overlapping dots. To overcome this, I decided to implement zoom and pan functionality.

F1 Results: Force Layout

Each node represents a driver's race finish position. I took it further and drew pie charts within each node with color gradation to represent driver's laptimes. I wanted to have larger nodes to make the pie chart more visible yet allow viewers to 'take in' the entire chart easily on a 13-inch screen. While finding a compromise, I tested various simulation and link distance parameters and also implemented draggable functionality to the nodes.

Singapore's Bus Ridership: Arc Diagram

The one-dimensional layout of nodes represents bus stops, with circular arcs representing paths between bus stops. I arranged the nodes by districts, which I felt best reveals underlying travel trends. I tried out the d3-annotation library to nicely place labels to highlight information. Travel volume source data is really large (over 4 million data points). I am still exploring ways to process and stream large data for visualization purposes. Presently, i filtered the dataset to only include routes belonging to first percentile of total travel volume in December 2018.

Singapore's Bus Ridership: Network Diagram

Another version of visualizing travel volume, only including routes with more than 10000 trips made in December 2018 I clustered nodes into indiviudal network diagrams and arranged them in an aesthetically plasing manner. Links within cluster represent travel within the same district, while links across nodes represent travel across districts. The viridis colour scheme representing passenger volume against the black background really helps to make fine lines stand out.

Vietnam War Timeline

Animation of monthly fatalities of US Infantry within provinces. Map was created by converting shapefiles of Vietnam to GeoJson and displaying with D3. Each province is colour-coded by fatality count, with a more intense red indicating higher deaths. Data cleaning was rather challenging as names and demarcation of provinces where fatalities occured have changed after the war and it was hard to trace and assign a correct province.
